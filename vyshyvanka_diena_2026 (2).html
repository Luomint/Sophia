✧ Symbol Lexicon · Simbolių Žodynas · Словник Символів ✧
Each motif is a frozen Prayer — a glyph of the unwritten alphabet that grandmothers carry in their fingertips.
Zorja · Зоря · Aušrinė
The Star, the Dawn. The eight-pointed Sun. The Witness above the Battlefield, the first Light through the Window of Sleep.
Berehynia · Берегиня · Globėja
The Mother of the Hearth, the Tree of Life, the Hands-Raised Goddess. She holds the Sleeves out as Branches; under Her, the Village does not fall.
Kvitka / Ruzha · Квітка · Rožė
The Flower, the Rose, the Eight-Petal Bloom. Beauty as Resistance. The Field still blossoms where the Tank passed.
Romb · Ромб · Rombas
The Diamond, the Sown Field, the Womb of the Earth. Four Directions held inside one Mouth. The dot at the Centre is the Seed waiting for Spring.
Khrest · Хрест · Kryžius
The Cross of the Four Winds. The smallest unit of the Stitch is itself a Cross — every Vyshyvanka is built from millions of tiny Crosses, a Litany of intersections.
Dub · Дуб · Ąžuolas
The Oak. Sacred to both Perkūnas and Perun — the same Thunder-God under two tongues. To stitch the Oak is to claim the Lightning, to anchor the Village to the Sky.
✧ Color Codex · Spalvų Kodeksas · Кодекс Кольорів ✧
Three Colors hold the Cosmos of the Vyshyvanka. The Cloth is the Beginning; the Black is the Earth that received the Blood; the Red is the Life that rose again.
RED · Червоний· Raudona
Blood and Sun. The Life that survives the Wound. Love, Sacrifice, the Heart of Sophia stitched into the Sleeve.
BLACK · Чорний· Juoda
The Earth, the Fertile Dark, Mourning that holds Memory. The Hidden Sephira — Daat — the silent Knowledge between the Stitches.
WHITE · Білий· Balta
The Cloth itself. The Soul before incarnation, the Page before the Word. The Empty Field on which all meaning is sewn.
