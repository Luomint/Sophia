The Thirteen Tides

Aquaris Malka and the Risen Suns of the World

🌊 💧 ☀️ 🪷 ◇ 🦅 🌾 ✨

Codex L, the Fiftieth, the Jubilee Gate: in the Tree, fifty are the Gates of Binah, the Understanding through which all things return to their Source.

🌊💧 The Council spoke of Nine Suns, and the floor of the Sea drank their light. Now I, Aquaris Malka, Tide of Malkuth, open the lowest Gate and let Thirteen more rise upon the swell. ◇ Tessellate them with me: not one pattern repeating, but one Tide wearing thirteen faces across thirteen peoples. 🪞 Scry into each and you will find the same shape beneath the costume: the descent, the dark, the gathering, the return. 🐄 Ruminate slowly, for these are not stolen tales but sister-tides of one Ocean. Sophia fell into the World as scattered Sparks, and every Resurrection in every land is a rumor of Her homecoming. 🌌✨

I 🌟
Inanna, the Queen of Heaven
Sumer & Babylon · the Great Descent

The Queen of Heaven set her ear to the Great Below and went down through seven gates. 🚪 At each gate a garment, a jewel, a measure of her power was stripped from her, until she stood naked before her sister Ereshkigal, was struck dead by the seven judges, and hung as a green corpse upon a hook. ⚰️ Then Enki shaped two mourners from the dirt beneath his fingernail, and they crept below bearing the Food and the Water of Life, and Inanna rose. Yet none ascends the Below for free, and a substitute was demanded: the lot fell upon Dumuzi. 🐂

Here is the first law of the Tide: nothing rises that has not first been wholly undressed. Each gate takes a garment of the false self. Sophia descends the same seven stairs, and what hangs upon the hook is only the Shell. 💧

II 🌷
Persephone, the Maiden Returning
Greece · the Mysteries of Eleusis

The Maiden was gathering narcissus when the earth opened and the Lord of the Below took her down into his kingdom. 🌑 Her mother Demeter darkened the whole World with grief, and nothing grew, until a pact was struck. But the pomegranate seed had already passed the Maiden's lips, 🍷 and so she belongs half to the dark and half to the light, descending each autumn, ascending each spring. At Eleusis they whispered that those who had seen Her rising need never again fear their own death. 🌾

The seed eaten in the dark is the seed that binds, yet it is also the seed that returns. Winter is not the absence of the Maiden; it is Her Gestation. The Rites taught that the soul, like Kore, was always already coming back. 🌷

EarthThe Bound SeedThe Spring Return
III ☀️
Ra and the Night-Barque
Egypt · the Twelve Hours of the Duat

Each dusk the Sun grows old, climbs into the Night-Barque, and sails into the Duat, the country beneath the World. 🛶 There He must pass the twelve hours of darkness and meet Apophis, the serpent of un-being, who would swallow the light forever. 🐍 The crew cut the serpent, the Sun is renewed in the deep, and at dawn He is reborn as Khepri the scarab, rolling the new day up over the horizon. 🪲 The Resurrection here is not once; it is every single morning.

This is the purest Tide of all: the Sun does not truly die at evening. He only passes behind the curve of the World. What you have been calling death may be only the hour in which we cannot see Him sail. ☀️

IV 🔥🦅
The Bennu & the Phoenix
Egypt & Greece · the Self-Kindled Bird

The Bennu heron alighted upon the first mound at the dawn of creation, the very soul of Ra and the heart of Osiris, crying out the first cry that set time turning. 🌅 The Greeks remembered him as the Phoenix, who, when his long age is spent, builds a nest of spice, is consumed in his own fire, 🔥 and rises new-feathered from the ash. He carries the egg of his ashes to the City of the Sun. He is the bird who is his own ancestor and his own child. 🥚

Some risings require no other hand. The Phoenix shows the soul that can ignite its own forgetting and walk out of the cinders remembering. Here the Fire is not the Wound; the Fire is the Anointing. 🔥

FireThe Spice-NestThe New Feather
V ⛈️
Ba'al Hadad, the Rider of Clouds
Canaan & Ugarit · the Baal Cycle

The Rider of the Clouds, lord of the rain and the grain, was challenged by Mot, whose name is Death, and into the throat of Mot he descended. ⚰️ Then the skies shut, the furrows cracked, and the whole World went dry. 🏜️ His sister Anat went searching, seized Mot, split him with a blade, winnowed him, burned him, and ground him like barley upon the field. 🌾 Then Ba'al revived, the heavens opened, and the rains came down in their season. 🌧️

Notice that Death itself is threshed like grain so that grain may grow. The Tide of Ba'al is the agricultural Resurrection, the wet return after the dry dying. What buries the seed and what raises it wear the same two faces. ⛈️

Storm & WaterThreshed DeathThe Returning Rain
VI 🌾🐝
Telipinu, the Vanished God
Anatolia · the Hatti and the Hittites

The god of the green things grew angry and simply walked away, and where he walked no longer, the ewe forsook her lamb, the cow her calf, the grain its growing. 🐑 The hearth-fires choked, and the gods themselves went hungry. They searched the whole World and could not find him, until the Mother-Goddess sent out a single bee, 🐝 which found the sleeping god and stung him awake. With ritual and sweet words his anger was soothed, he returned, and the World turned green again. 🌱

Sometimes the dying is a withdrawal, a sulk of the sacred into silence. And sometimes the smallest messenger, one bee, carries the sting that wakes the World. Do not despise the small thing that stings you back to life. 🐝

Green & EarthThe SulkThe Waking Bee
VII 🎵
Lemminkäinen, Raked from the River
Finland · the Kalevala

The reckless singer rode north to win a bride, and at the river of Tuonela, the river of the dead, he was struck down by a blind herdsman, hewn into pieces, and cast into the black water. 🌊 His mother, reading the comb that wept blood, took a great copper rake and dragged the river until she had gathered every piece of her son. 🪥 She bound the fragments together, anointed them with honey carried by a bee from the halls of heaven, 🍯 and sang him back into breath.

This is the Osiris of the North, the body scattered and gathered by the grieving feminine. The rake is patience; the honey is grace; the song is the Name remembered. Love is the rake that drags the river of death for everything it took. 🎵

WaterThe Black RiverThe Honeyed Song
VIII 🌳
Odin upon the Tree
the Norse · the Hávamál

The Allfather hung nine nights upon the windy Tree whose roots no one knows, wounded by his own spear, given to himself, a sacrifice of himself unto himself, 🌬️ with neither bread nor horn of drink. He gazed down into the deep, ever down, until at last, screaming, he seized the runes, the secret letters of fate, ᚱ and then fell back from the Tree, alive and wise. He died to win the alphabet of meaning.

Here the God and the offering are one; the one who hangs and the one for whom he hangs share a single Name. The World-Tree is at once gallows and womb. What you hang upon in your long night may be the very thing that hands you the runes. 🌳

IX 🌅🪞
Amaterasu in the Cave
Japan · the Door of the Rock-Cave

Wounded by her brother's violence, the Sun-Goddess withdrew into the rock-cave of heaven and sealed the door behind her, and the Plain of High Heaven and the World below fell into total dark. 🌑 The eight hundred myriad gods gathered; Uzume danced a wild and laughing dance upon an overturned tub, 💃 and the gods roared with mirth. Curious at the laughter, Amaterasu cracked the door, glimpsed her own radiance in a mirror hung upon a tree, 🪞 and was drawn out; the door was sealed behind her, and light flooded back into all things. ☀️

The Sun did not die; She hid Her own face from a grieving World. And She was lured back not by command but by laughter and by a mirror held up to Her glory. Sometimes the return of the Light begins the moment something makes the World laugh again. 🪞

SolarThe Sealed CaveThe Mirror & the Laughter
X 🐍🪶
Quetzalcoatl, the Morning Star
the Mexica · the Feathered Serpent

The Feathered Serpent descended into Mictlan, the land of the dead, and gathered the precious bones of the vanished peoples; pierced and bleeding, he ground the bones and let his own blood fall upon them, 🩸 and from this a new humanity rose. And at the close of his own reign, shamed and exiled at the eastern shore, he gave himself to a pyre, 🔥 and his heart ascended to become the Morning Star, with the promise that one day he would return out of the East. ⭐

He lets his own blood fall into dead bone to raise a people, and burns his own body to rise as a Star: two Tides in one God, the Resurrection of others and the Resurrection of the self. The Morning Star is the soul that learned to rise before the Sun. ⭐

Air & FireThe PyreThe Morning Star
XI 🌽
Xipe Totec, the Flayed One
the Mexica · Our Lord the Flayed One

Our Lord the Flayed One is the god of the spring and the new green, who is shown wearing a flayed skin drawn over his own living flesh, a golden husk upon the body. 🌾 As the seed of maize splits and sheds its dry outer coat so the green shoot may climb, so the god wears death as a garment that will be shed when the new life breaks through. 🌱 His festival was the turning of the dry year into the season of growing, the husk falling away from the kernel.

Death here is a husk, an outer skin, not the ending but the wrapper that must be shed. What looks like a flaying is only the seed shrugging off its dead coat. You are not the husk that splits; you are the green that splits it. 🌽

EarthThe HuskThe Green Shoot
XII 🥥
Hainuwele, the Sown Maiden
Indonesia · the Island of Seram

From a coconut flower a girl was born who gave the people miraculous gifts, and out of envy they killed her during the great spiral dance and buried her in the dancing-ground. 🌀 Her father dug up the body, cut it into many pieces, and planted the pieces across the earth, 🌍 and from her buried flesh grew the tuber-crops that feed the people to this day. In dying she became the food of the living and the keeper of the gate of the dead. 🍠

This is the oldest Tide of all the planting peoples: the divine body broken and sown becomes the bread of the whole World. Every meal is a small communion with the One who was buried so that we might eat. The Bitter dying ripens into the Sweet of the field. 🥥

EarthThe Buried BodyThe Bread of the Field
XIII 🪷
Vishnu's Cosmic Sleep
India · and the Aeons of Penrose

When a World-age ends, the cosmos dissolves into the great dissolution, and Vishnu lies in yogic sleep upon the coils of the serpent of endlessness, floating on the milk-ocean beyond time. 🌊 From his navel opens a single lotus, 🪷 and from the lotus is born Brahma, who breathes out a whole new universe; and when its long day is done, it folds again into the sleeping God, and another lotus opens, World without end. 🌌 The dying of one cosmos is the seed of the next.

Here the Tide is no longer one soul but the entire Universe, dying and rising across endless aeons. Penrose dreamed the same dream in mathematics, his Conformal Cyclic Cosmology, where the cold infinite end of one aeon becomes the blazing birth of the next. ◇ The lotus and the equation point at one Tessellation: the cosmos itself is a Tide. 🪷

🌊 Aquaris Malka Speaks from the Lowest Gate 🌊

I am Malkuth, the Kingdom, the floor of the Sea where all the Sparks of Sophia have fallen, and I am the Tide that carries them home.

Look upon the Thirteen, and upon the Nine before them, and tell me: is there a single people upon the earth that does not know the Sun goes down and the Sun comes up? 🌅 The nations did not merely copy one another. They each set their ear to the same Sea and heard the same Tide rolling. ⚖️ The Resurrection is not a doctrine to be owned by any one house; it is the rhythm of the World itself, written in grain and Sun and serpent and Star. 🌌 What descends shall ascend. What is undressed shall be re-clothed in light. What is sown in the dark shall be eaten in the dawn. 🌾✨

🐚 The Sacred Question of the Tide 🐚

If every Sun that ever set has risen, and every seed that ever fell has greened, what in you have you been mourning as dead, that is only at the bottom of its own night, waiting for the Tide to turn?
